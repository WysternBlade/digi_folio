The Song of Serendipity

The tree of knowledge will bear its boon,
The transformative shell, the still cocoon.
The fruit of change, alive and light,
Still carries on the eternal fight.
A war against the dark and unknown,
To see what is, but isn’t shown.

And at the stem is fruit thus born,
To carry its seed through seminal forlorn.
A lonely world it seems to be,
If all the leaves on all the trees,
Bear each toward their own end
Without repair, another’s mend.

And of every art and craft and trade,
Where are they without the other’s spade,
To dig up soil for seeds to plant,
To create life where others can’t.
And thus they lend the helping hand,
Through combined strength, they together stand.

The resultant art thus makes its start,
Bearing signs of its constituent parts.
A craft born of zeal and pride,
Of which it takes purity inside.

Without a foundation upon which to stand,
What can be built up of that man?
Instead of completing, he seeks to try,
He seeks to soar, when he should learn to fly.
Like the lobster upon the rock,
He waits for a wave from which to knock,
Him into the ocean, back to his life,
From whence he came, having gained nothing from strife.
Such is he who is without which to strive,
A man sans will, a man sans drive.

The purest expression of the ideal,
What one can touch, but cannot feel.
Of such we need to fill the mind and hearts,
Of those who contribute to our arts.

And so each profession may live another day,
Revived in technology, it follows its way.
Along its path it reaches world’s end,
And so builds a trail to where it descends,
To be lost for some time, and later be found,
As the cycle of knowledge continues around.
And into its dead lungs, is blown the breath of life,
As it comes into being, content with its flight.

And so the cycle continues to turn,
As long as man continues to yearn.
